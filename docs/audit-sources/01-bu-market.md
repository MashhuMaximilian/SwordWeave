## 🎯 PURPOSE

The **BU Market** defines a shared construction language for building Capabilities and modular systems within the engine. It provides:

- **The Blueprint Catalog:** A granular directory of universal building blocks (detailed in later sections).
- **The Weight Continuum:** A strict tier system evaluating structural weight rather than absolute numeric power balance.
- **The Progression Blueprint:** A guideline-based BU model that scales alongside character level development.
- **The Open Foundry:** A standardized protocol for designing custom components cleanly.
- **The Adaptable Frame:** A flexible, DM-centric framework that shifts contextually based on setting logic.

> ⚠️ **Core Axiom:** The Market is a structural design language, not a hardcoded legal rule enforcement system.

## 🧠 SYSTEM POSITION

The **BU Market** serves as the definitive mechanical bridge positioned explicitly between the engine's core layers:

```
[ BU System & Rules ]  ---> [ THE BU MARKET ] ---> [ Capability Construction ]
   (Defines Currency)           (The Language)         (Builds Living Entities)
```

### 🛑 What it does NOT define:

- **Combat Resolution:** The situational math of dynamic execution loops.
- **Numerical Power Balance:** Flat power thresholds or vertical optimization tracking.
- **Gameplay Outcomes:** The narrative finality of player choices at the table.

## ⚙️ CORE DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

- **Guidelines, Not Absolutes:** Primitives are intentional benchmarks; the DM shapes their final operational expression.
- **Recommendations Over Law:** BU values denote architectural complexity, not absolute tactical pricing.
- **Universal Vetting:** All core subsystems remain dynamically DM-adjustable at all execution phases.
- **Expressive Parity:** **Creativity overrides rigidity.** This engine actively prioritizes flexible, narrative-driven expressive construction over strict mathematical optimization.

## 🧱 COMPONENT TIER SYSTEM

The blueprint architecture maps all primitives into five distinct structural layers, anchored by their developmental weight:

- **🟢 Tier 1 — Minor Components (1–2 BU Anchor):** Light structural elements, basic resource nodes, and low-impact modifications.
- **🟡 Tier 2 — Standard Components (3–5 BU / 4 BU Anchor):** Core building blocks used in the vast majority of standard capabilities, including standard Practice modifiers.
- **🟠 Tier 3 — Major Components (6–10 / 8 BU Anchor):** Build-defining mechanics, high-impact tactical shifts, and probability manipulation tracks.
- **🔴 Tier 4 — Core Axes (11–20+ BU Anchor):** Foundational primitives, massive mechanical rule-breaks, and reality-defining axes that dictate what builds can exist.
- **🟣 Tier 5 — Narrative Layer (Outside BU Constraints):** Fully DM-defined cosmic constructs, environmental rules, and timeline overrides completely separated from character ledger progression.

## ⚖️ BU PRICING MODEL (GUIDELINE LOGIC)

> 💡 **The Core Principle:** BU cost reflects **structural complexity**, not absolute tactical power.

When determining or adjusting the structural cost of a component, the price is dynamically influenced by six universal engineering factors:

1. **Compositional Complexity:** The total number of internal sub-elements packed into a single atomic asset.
1. **System Synergy:** The total amount of distinct engine tracks or mechanical resolution loops the component directly interfaces with.
1. **Scope Scale:** The spatial footprint of the asset:
  - *Self / Coordinate Space* → Low Weight
  - *Single Target* → Low Weight
  - *Area Footprint* → Medium Weight
  - *Battlefield Scale* → High Weight
  - *Reality / Timeline Scale* → Tier 4 Core Axis
1. **Temporal Structure:** The baseline temporal footprint of the application:
  - *Instantaneous* → Low Weight
  - *Sustained Track* → Medium Weight
  - *Permanent Frame* → High Weight
1. **Control Impact:** Cost dynamically escalates if the asset actively overrides target agency, forces mathematical outcomes, or explicitly rewrites core resolution rules.
1. **Stackability Matrix:** * *Non-Stackable Nodes* → Lower Cost
  - *Stackable Modifiers* → Higher Cost
  - *Exponential/Multiplying Stacks* → High Tier 3 or Tier 4 Axis

## 🧩 SHAPE & FORM SYSTEM

Shapes in this engine are **interpretive spatial descriptors**, not rigid geometric constraints. They serve to map out the visual narrative footprint of a capability across coordinate slots.

### 📐 Standard Formats

- **Point / Coordinate:** Direct single-slot target focus.
- **Line / Vector Path:** Linear trajectory passing through progressive coordinate slots.
- **Cone:** Flared spatial projection expanding outward from a primary direction.
- **Sphere / Radius:** Uniform omnidirectional expansion centered on a fixed coordinate.
- **Ring / Halo:** Circular perimeter boundary that leaves its internal center unaffected.
- **Wall / Plane:** Rigid linear barrier blocking or altering line-of-sight tracking.
- **Zone / Field:** Persistent tactical environment anchoring over a group of coordinates.
- **Chain:** Conversational or kinetic link leaping dynamically from target to target.

### 🌌 Narrative Formats (Interpretive Spatial Breaks)

- **Spiral Field:** Whirling, cyclical vectors dragging coordinates toward a center axis.
- **Star / Bloom Expansion:** Radiating spikes of energy or organic growth rupturing outward.
- **Fractured Zone / Folding Space:** Non-contiguous clusters of coordinates affected simultaneously, or space compressed together.
- **Layered Zones:** Vertically or dimensionally stacked tactical environments overlapping the same coordinate footprint.
- **Organic / Freeform Shapes:** Malleable, fluid boundaries flowing naturally to fit local environmental terrain.

> ⚠️ **The Layout Principle:** A shape primitive *only* escalates in BU cost if it expands the capability's operational **Scope Scale** (Targeting density), introduces complex environmental control properties, or increases systemic interaction friction.

## ⚔️ INTERACTION RULES

Primitives function like independent programming nodes. When layered together within a composed template ledger, they interact through a definitive hierarchy managed dynamically by the DM:

- **Stack:** Primitives aggregate their numerical values or overlay concurrent states onto a target.
- **Replace / Overwrite:** High-tier primitives completely erase and substitute the operational rules of low-tier primitives.
- **Cancel:** Directly opposing structural tags (e.g., matching positive and negative elemental attributes) neutralize one another.
- **Amplify:** Strategic synergy hooks cause one primitive component to step up the magnitude tier or scale block of another.
- **Trigger Conditionally:** The component rests dormant on the ledger until specific runtime environment conditions are satisfied.

> 📝 **Design Obligation:** All structural interactions between complex primitives must be explicitly defined and visual within the final composed Capability Template to ensure clean runtime performance at the table.

## 🔄 Negative Primitives: The Mirror-Vector Architecture

The **Mirror-Vector Architecture** eliminates the need for a separate, cluttered \"Disadvantages or Flaws\" table. Instead, it introduces a completely unified, symmetrical character ledger where valid mathematical or defensive primitives available in the standard BU Market can be inverted to yield an immediate Build Unit credit.

### I. The Core Principle of Inversion

Character growth in this engine is balanced through structural stability. A player does not \"purchase\" an external flaw archetype; they simply invert a component's operational vector directly on their master character ledger.

- **Standard Vector (+BU):** Unlocks a baseline benefit or permission, increasing the global sum of spent BU (e.g., purchasing a $+2$ modifier to a Practice costs **+4 BU**).
- **Mirror Vector (-BU):** Voluntarily accepts a structural drawback or vulnerability, subtracting that cost from the global sum of spent BU and instantly freeing up currency for other parts of the build (e.g., accepting a permanent $-2$ penalty to a Practice grants a **4 BU** credit).

### II. Mirror Eligibility: Metrics vs. Permissions

To maintain systemic engine stability, primitives are divided into two distinct vector categories regarding their ability to be mirrored:

### 📊 The Variable Vector (Mirrorable Nodes)

These components modify the raw mathematical weight and numerical outputs of the engine. Inverting them creates a visible \"Structural Friction\" that is easily calculated as a direct BU credit:

- **Numerical Metrics (Sheet Baseline):** Flat penalties applied to structural Practices (e.g., $-2$ to *Awareness*), Attribute Increments (e.g., $-1$ to *Mental*), Attack/Accuracy Tracks, or Dynamic Character DCs (e.g., $-1$ to *Focused Presence*).
- **Vitality Blocks:** Intentionally lowering the baseline calculation of the character's Max Vitality pool (e.g., inverting *Vitality Core Augment I* to suffer a $-5$ Max HP penalty yields a **4 BU** credit).
- **Probability Bias Tracks:** Accepting permanent **Negative Bias** (Disadvantage) across a designated Practice axis, defensive check, or resolution gate (e.g., permanent Disadvantage on all *Reason* saves grants a **6 BU** credit).
- **Structural Faults:** Inverting an entry from the *Structural Defensive Market*. Instead of purchasing protection or resistance against a specific payload, the player introduces a permanent vulnerability (such as taking double damage magnitude or automatically failing initial saves) against that domain's effects (**4 to -8 BU**).
- **Kinematic Metrics (Movement Speed):** Inverting the *Stride Extension* primitive. Instead of gaining extra movement, the character accepts a flat $-10\\text{ ft}$ reduction to their baseline movement speed, yielding a **5 BU** credit.
- **Strain & Cost Buffers:** Inverting mitigation primitives from the *Evaluation & Strain Market* to make the character's framework highly unstable:
  - *Fragile Intent:* Inverting a *Heuristic Buffer* (which normally reduces Strain by 1) forces the character to take $+1$ additional Strain whenever they execute a capability, granting a **12 BU** credit.
  - *Metaphysical Debt:* Inverting a *Vitality Shielding* node forces the character to pay double ($2\\times$) the upfront Vitality costs for executing high-strain actions, granting a **10 BU** credit.

### 🏛️ The Permission Vector (Non-Mirrorable Nodes)

These components grant fundamental conceptual rights, semantic language permissions, structural scaling limits, or spatial formatting frameworks. They **cannot** be inverted because possessing "negative access" to a concept or rule-license is logically invalid within the engine:

- **Lexicon Tiers:** Verbs (Action permissions like *Strike* or *Create*) and Domains (Thematic reality licenses like *Fire*, *Kinetic*, or *Void*).
- **Intensity Blocks (Damage/Healing Dice):** Primitives that unlock payload expressions (e.g., *Impact Die Block*, *Calamity Die Block*, *Existential Tear*). You cannot roll "negative dice" or deal "negative damage blocks" to gain currency; scaling down intensity simply means choosing not to purchase a higher tier.
- **Spatial & Targeting Logic:** Range Gates (*Close*, *Far*), Sizing Templates (*Cones*, *Radius*, *Lines*), and advanced structural targeting permissions like *Collateral Buffer* (Friendly Fire immunity) or *Selective Focus*.
- **Chronological & Temporal Paths (Durations):** Execution speeds (*Fast/Instant Quickening*) and duration licenses (*Sustained*, *Scene*, *Permanent*). Time and persistence cannot be mathematically flipped; you cannot possess a "negative permanent" duration.
- **System Bypasses (Qualitative Unlocks):** Absolute sensory or mobility rights that bypass the normal laws of the environment, such as *Flight*, *Darkvision*, *Tremorsense*, *Phase-Slip*, or additional *Reaction Slots*.
- **Runtime Trigger Hooks:** The passive "monitoring sensors" that allow a capability to dynamically fire out-of-sequence (e.g., *Reactive Guard* or *Causality Interdiction*). These are qualitative structural logic gates that cannot be inverted.
- **Semantic State Tags:** The foundational permissions required to apply conditions or interact with specific facets of reality (e.g., *Physical Interaction* or *Cognitive & Agency* tags). These define *what* your intent is legally allowed to touch, and cannot be set to a negative.

### 🛠️ The "Exposure" Safety Valve (Vetting Reminder)

**Remember the** **Exposure Rule**: A player only receives BU credit for a mirrored node if the DM can reasonably "expose" that flaw during play. For example, a "Structural Fault" against a rare or non-existent domain in your campaign (like "Vulnerability to Narrative Truth" in a dungeon crawl) should be rejected during vetting as it provides no actual **Structural Friction**.

### III. The Tier-Matched Volatility Ceiling (Balancing Logic)

To prevent early-game "glass cannon" min-max exploits, a character's total accumulated debt from negative primitives is strictly capped based on their current leveling career and tier access:

| **Character Level** | **Accessible Lexicon Tier** | **Max Volatility Ceiling** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Levels 1–4** | Tier I & II (Minor / Standard) | **-8 BU** |
| **Levels 5–10** | Tier III (Major) | **-12 BU** |
| **Levels 11–15** | Tier IV (Core Axes) | **-16 BU** |
| **Levels 16+** | Tier IV+ (Apex Tier) | **-24 BU** |

*Note: Characters at early levels cannot take high-tier, reality-breaking flaws because they lack the structural baseline to harbor that much operational debt. Characters extending into post-level 20 epic play may scale these caps further under direct DM oversight.*

### IV. The Volatility Rating (DM Snapshot)

Negative primitives are written directly into the standard ledger alongside positive ones, requiring no secondary bookkeeping or math pools. However, the absolute sum of all negative primitives is noted as a metadata reference value:

$$\text{Volatility Rating} = \sum |\text{Negative Primitives}|$$

This rating serves as a tactical map for the DM. A high Volatility Rating alerts the DM exactly which mechanical liabilities and structural gaps can be leveraged during high-stakes encounters to create maximum narrative tension.

### V. The Exposure Rule (Vetting)

A Mirror Node only grants a BU credit if it introduces genuine **Structural Friction** within the current campaign context.

- **Vetting Protocol:** The DM must approve every negative primitive. If a player takes a heavy structural fault against an incredibly rare domain that the DM does not intend to use in the current setting (e.g., a vulnerability to the *Void* domain in a street-level crime drama), the BU credit is denied or reduced to 0.
- **Operational Requirement:** The DM is narratively and mechanically justified in ensuring every active flaw on a character ledger is targeted and "exposed" through the engine's resolution events at least once per session arc.

## 🧩 HOW TO DESIGN A BU PRIMITIVE (ATOMIC PROTOCOL)

This design protocol is used to add a brand-new "brick" directly to the global **BU Market catalog**. These elements serve as the raw, atomic ingredients referenced in the [🧠 Capability Composition Map (Canonical)](https://app.notion.com/p/37fed8479ccd810dbd98e4c942a98553)

### STEP 1 — IDENTIFY THE ATOMIC AXIS

A primitive component must fit cleanly into exactly **one** of the four "Fundamental Building Blocks." If a proposed design tries to merge more than one axis, it is an integrated *Capability*, not a *Primitive*, and must be broken down.

1. **Lexicon (Verb):** Unlocks a new "Action Permission" or semantic movement vector within the system rules (e.g., *Invert*, *Synchronize*, *Strike*).
1. **Lexicon (Domain):** Unlocks a new "Slice of Reality" or elemental attunement theme (e.g., *Gravity*, *Fire*, *Causality*).
1. **Metric (Math):** Applies a flat, static numerical adjustment to character properties (e.g., *+2 to Finesse*, *+5 Max Vitality*).
1. **Permission (Rule-Break):** Permanently unlocks a specific physical or mechanical engine bypass (e.g., *Darkvision*, *Reaction Slot*, *Flight*).

### STEP 2 — DEFINE SYSTEMIC OPERATIONAL MEANING

Clearly define which specific engine track or "system knob" this primitive turns at runtime:

- Does it interact with **Resolution** (Practice Checks, Attack Mods, Dynamic Character DCs, Probability Tracks)?
- Does it interact with the **Action Economy** (Turn Tracks, Out-of-Sequence Timing triggers)?
- Does it interact with **Spatial Locomotion** (Coordinate Steps, Projected Vector paths)?
- Does it interact with the **Evaluation Layer** (Strain Tiers, Complexity Overhead thresholds)?

### STEP 3 — ASSIGN STRUCTURAL TIER (REALITY WEIGHT)

Assign a definitive structural tier based on how "deep" this element interacts with the engine's baseline source code. Use the standard market pricing anchors to determine its foundational cost guidelines:

- **Tier 1 (Minor — 1–2 BU Anchor):** Simple, tangible changes or minor numerical adjustments (e.g., *Kinetic*, *+5 Vitality*).
- **Tier 2 (Standard — 3–5 / 4 BU Anchor):** Core systemic changes, baseline Practice modifiers, or standard biological triggers (e.g., *Life*, *Reaction Slots*, *Practice Modifiers*).
- **Tier 3 (Major — 6–10 / 8 BU Anchor):** Abstract or highly conceptual shifts, advanced probability adjustments, and systemic track overrides (e.g., *Intent*, *Probability/Bias Manipulation*).
- **Tier 4 (Core Axis — 11–20+ BU Anchor):** Reality-defining rule breaks and baseline architecture rewrites (e.g., *Causality*, *Timeline Anchors*, *Boss Economy Cadences*).

### STEP 4 — THE ATOMIC VECTOR TOGGLE (INVERSION CHECK)

Determine if this specific primitive can be safely flagged as a **Mirror Node** (Negative BU Credit) when written onto a character ledger. Apply the crisp binary vector filter:

- **📊 Variable Vector ➔ [MIRRORABLE = TRUE]:** If the component alters a numeric baseline (Metrics), manipulates resolution math (Probability tracks), or reinforces structural limits (Defenses), it can be freely inverted. *Designer Task: Document the mechanical downside if the sign is flipped to a negative (e.g., Inverting a Tier II Practice Modifier grants -4 BU and inflicts a permanent -2 penalty to the track).*
- **🏛️ Permission Vector ➔ [MIRRORABLE = FALSE]:** If the component grants a conceptual license, an action Verb, a spatial format gate, or a qualitative trait bypass, mark it as **Permission Only**. It cannot be inverted to yield currency under any circumstances.

### STEP 5 — VALIDATION (THE ATOMICITY TEST)

Run the newly designed primitive through the final validation filter. If the answer to any of these three validation checkpoints is **"Yes"**, the component is too complex and must be rejected and split back into separate primitives:

1. **Does it define its own Targeting constraints?** *(No. Targeting layout is a separate Structure primitive).*
1. **Does it define its own Range gate?** *(No. Range scaling is a separate Resolution primitive).*
1. **Does it define its own Damage Die or payload magnitude?** *(No. Damage intensity scaling is a separate Intensity primitive).*

## 🧑‍⚖️ DM OVERRIDE PRINCIPLE

The DM remains the ultimate **Translation Engine** of the architecture. To ensure narrative settings dictate mechanical reality, the DM possesses the direct structural authority to:

- **Recalibrate Weights:** Adjust individual BU pricing guidelines up or down based on specific campaign environments.
- **Mutate Primitives:** Redefine the systemic operational meaning or categorical execution of any existing component.
- **Refactor Ledger Entries:** Merge overlapping primitives together or split a complex element into simpler, separate atoms.
- **Bypass Tier Constraints:** Entirely ignore baseline component tier caps to accommodate specialized progression tracks.
- **Introduce Primitives:** Manufacture completely new, narrative-driven reality constructs directly into the market.

> 💡 **The Core Mandate:** The BU Market is a generative framework designed to unleash player creativity, not a hard constraint engineered to bottleneck it.

## 🧠 DESIGN INTENT

The structural engine of the **BU Market** exists explicitly to realize five systemic goals:

1. **A Shared Vocabulary:** Establishing a clean, universal language that lets players and DMs clearly communicate the composition of their ideas.
1. **Modular Architecture:** Guaranteeing that every single system layer remains decoupled, swappable, and entirely reusable.
1. **Expressive Agency:** Empowering players to build completely unique, customized capability frameworks without being restricted by rigid class archetypes.
1. **Preservation of Authority:** Leaving final system interpretation, world balance, and context filters firmly in the hands of the DM.
1. **Emergent Complexity:** Allowing deep, rich tactical dynamics to naturally bloom out of simple, easily tracked atomic rules.

## 🧾 FINAL PRINCIPLE

> ⚖️ **The Golden Law:** The BU Market is an open language for **constructing creative ideas**, not a restrictive lawbook for enforcing mathematical outcomes. Always prioritize the translation of logic and intent over the rigid optimization of code.

## 📌 Note on Atomicity Test Scope

The **Atomicity Test (STEP 5)** validates that no **single primitive** in the BU Market catalog is doing the work of two. It does NOT apply to **assembled capabilities**, which correctly compose multiple primitives (Verb + Domain + Range + Effects + Triggers + Duration + Targeting) into a single capability card.

When designing a primitive, ask: "Is this one atomic action?" When designing a capability, ask: "Which primitives combine to express this intent?" Both questions are valid at their respective scopes.
