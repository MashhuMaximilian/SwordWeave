# 🧠 Capability Upkeep & Interruption (Canonical)

## Core Identity

Capability Upkeep is the system for sustaining ongoing capabilities in play.

It replaces any need for a separate concentration mechanic.

---

# Core Principles

A capability can exist in three states:

- Instant (resolves immediately)
- Duration-based (exists for a fixed time)
- Maintained (requires upkeep)

Upkeep represents the ongoing cost required to keep a capability active in reality.

---

# When Does Upkeep Exist?

Upkeep applies only when a capability is explicitly sustained beyond its initial resolution.

Examples include:

- Barriers and shields
- Summoned entities
- Ongoing zones or fields
- Transformations
- Persistent environmental effects
- Any capability designed with maintenance behavior

Even normally instant effects can be designed with upkeep if constructed that way.

---

# Upkeep Timing

Upkeep is paid at the start of the user's turn.

If the upkeep cost is paid:

- The effect continues normally

If the upkeep cost is not paid:

- The effect immediately ends

---

# Conditions That Break Upkeep

The following conditions automatically end maintained effects:

- Unconscious
- Incapacitated
- Stunned
- Petrified
- Dead

Only these apply by default unless the DM explicitly extends the rule for specific effects.

---

# Explicit Disruption Capabilities

Some capabilities are designed to interfere with or remove ongoing effects:

- Dispel-type effects
- Countering capabilities
- Silence or anti-magic interference
- Nullification fields
- Other designated disruption abilities

These resolve through normal capability vs DC resolution rules.

---

# Damage Pressure Rule (Upkeep Disruption Layer)

When a creature maintaining a capability takes damage during a turn:

If:

> Total damage taken this turn ≥ upkeep cost of maintained effects

Then:

- The user must immediately re-pay the upkeep cost

Outcomes:

- If they pay → the effect continues
- If they cannot pay → the effect ends

This is not a roll and not a save.

It is a resource pressure interaction rule.

Damage is aggregated per turn for this check.

---

# Casting Interruption Window

Capabilities that require execution time may be interrupted only during their casting window.

Casting windows include:

- 1 action or turn-based casting
- multi-round casting
- minute-based casting
- ritual casting

If interruption occurs during the window, the DM determines outcome based on progress:

- No cost incurred
- Partial cost incurred
- Full cost incurred

Instant execution capabilities cannot be interrupted.

---

# Duration Expiration

If a capability has a fixed duration:

- When the duration ends, the effect ends automatically

No additional rules apply.

---

# Multiple Maintained Effects

A character may maintain multiple capabilities simultaneously.

There is no hard limit.

Instead:

- Upkeep costs accumulate
- Resource pressure increases
- Strain increases across active capabilities

---

# Design Model

The system uses:

- Vitality (resource pool)
- Strain (execution pressure)
- Cost (consequence system)
- Capability structure (effect definition)

Maintained effects are governed entirely by these systems.

---

# Interruption Summary

A maintained capability can end only through:

1. Failure to pay upkeep
1. Breaking conditions
1. Explicit disruption capabilities
1. Damage pressure rule failure
1. Natural duration ending
1. DM adjudication in exceptional cases
