## 🧱 1. Space & Intent: Cover vs. Execution Method

### Core Principle

Cover is not a permanent modification to a character sheet; it is a **temporary spatial trait applied to a specific coordinate grid square**. It represents an obstacle physically intercepting a trajectory line. Because this architecture runs on three **Source Types** (Physical, Magical, Psychic), a spatial barrier only penalizes or blocks an action if its physical composition can logically interact with how the capability is delivered.

### The Two States of Spatial Cover

- **Total Cover:** An obstacle completely severs the direct line of sight and execution vector between the attacker's coordinate and the target's coordinate.
  - *Systemic Rule:* Projected direct attacks cannot target this coordinate. Line of sight is considered broken.
- **Half Cover:** An obstacle partially obstructs the trajectory line but does not completely sever line of sight (e.g., a tree trunk, a low stone wall, or an ally standing in the way).
  - *Systemic Rule:* The attacker suffers a flat accuracy penalty when attempting to project a vector at this coordinate.
  - **Standard Half Cover:** Flat **4 penalty** to the attacker's accuracy roll.
  - **Minor Obstruction (DM Discretion):** Flat **2 penalty** to the attacker's accuracy roll.

### 📡 The Execution Match: Projected Vectors vs. Direct Manifestations

To determine if cover applies, the DM filters the action into one of two execution behaviors:

### A. Projected Vectors (Arrows, Firebolts, Kinetic Rays, Shockwaves)

The capability relies on a physical projectile, beam, or concussive energy traveling through the intervening space from Point A to Point B.

- *Cover Rule:* **Full Cover Rules Apply.** Trajectories are completely blocked by Total Cover and suffer flat accuracy penalties (-4/-2) from Half Cover.

### B. Direct Manifestations (Gaze Combustion, Spatial Anchors, Psychic Waves)

The capability has no traveling flight path. The caster uses their purchased primitive licenses (such as a *Create* Verb + *Fire* Domain) to **instantiate the effect directly at the target coordinate** (e.g., causing an enemy's armor to spontaneously burst into flames, or warping the gravity directly beneath their feet).

- *Cover Rule:* **Ignores Spatial Cover Penalties.** Because nothing travels through the air, obstacles like trees or low walls offer **0 protection** against the strike.
- *The Only Gate:* The caster must possess a baseline **Line of Sight / Sensory Lock** to know the target occupies that coordinate space. If an enemy is fully hidden behind a mountain (Total Cover), they cannot be targeted. But if they are peeking out from Half Cover, a Direct Manifestation strikes them with zero accuracy penalties.

### ⚔️ Source Type General Behavior

- **Physical Sources:** Almost strictly *Projected Vectors* (respects cover).
- **Psychic Sources:** Strictly *Direct Manifestations* (ignores physical cover completely, requires only cognitive/sensory awareness).
- **Magical Sources:** Dynamic. A projected ball of magical energy is a vector (respects cover penalties). A gaze that causes an enemy's blood to boil is a manifestation (ignores cover penalties, requires only raw line of sight to the coordinate).

## 💀 2. Collapse & Trauma: The Unconsciousness Clock

### Core Principle

This architecture eliminates rigid mechanical bookkeeping, "death ticks," or automated d20 death saves when a character's life force fails. Instead, dropping to **0 Vitality** shifts the session into an active narrative emergency governed by a contextual timeline and player-driven creative problem solving.

### Hitting 0 Vitality

When an entity's current Vitality pool is reduced exactly to **0**, their system enters shock. They collapse, fall unconscious, and are fully incapacitated on their coordinate grid square.

- **The Heuristic Clock:** The fallen character is placed on a loose, thematic countdown window—typically tracking roughly **one minute** (approximated as 10 rounds of a active combat track) before their physical framework permanently expires.
- **The DM Appraisal Rule:** The DM determines the exact speed, pressure, and duration of this clock on the fly based on the heuristic context of the scene:
  - *Stable Clock:* A character knocked to 0 Vitality by a blunt martial punch or minor concussion receives a generous, stable timeline to be rescued.
  - *Volatile Clock:* A character dropped by a deep, bleeding laceration, an extreme burning domain payload, or exposure to hazardous environments (like freezing water) suffers a heavily accelerated, high-pressure timeline.

### 🩺 Stabilization Methods

To halt the countdown and save a fallen character, an ally must actively dedicate an action track to stabilize them before the narrative clock expires.

1. **The Standard Practice:** An ally executes a standard **Fieldcraft Check** utilizing their global Proficiency Bonus (PB) and relevant attributes to physically patch the wounds or apply basic trauma control.
1. **Creative Primitive Implementation:** Players are encouraged to look at their ledger of globally purchased primitives and invent improvised medical solutions on the fly.
- *The Play Style:* A player declares an action: *"I don't have a healing capability, but I own the Restrict Verb and the Ice Domain. Can I flash-freeze their open artery shut to stall the hemorrhage?"*
- *The Resolution:* The DM appraises the creative intent, evaluates the narrative complexity, sets an appropriate check or charges an upfront **Strain toll** to the caster, and rules whether the makeshift solution successfully stops the character's death clock.
- **Failure:** If the clock expires without a successful stabilization or medical intervention, the character dies permanently.

## 💥 3. The Massive Damage Boundary: Existential Shatter

### Core Principle

When the sheer numeric force of an incoming capability vastly overloads a character’s maximum structural capacity in a single strike, the unconsciousness timeline is bypassed entirely.

- **The Threshold Rule:** If a single capability execution deals damage that **equals or exceeds double (2×) the target's Maximum Vitality Pool** (or reduces their current pool to a negative integer equal to their Max Vitality), their physical or metaphysical matrix is instantaneously obliterated.
- **The Systemic Outcome:** The entity suffers an **Existential Shatter**. They do not fall unconscious to bleed out on a narrative clock; their framework is completely torn apart. **The character dies instantly on impact.**

> ⚠️ **Design Context:** This rule preserves high-stakes tactical danger. It ensures low-level characters naturally respect apex entities or devastating environmental hazards (like falling from terminal heights or being caught in an artillery strike) without requiring extra scaling numbers or bloated HP tracking tables.
